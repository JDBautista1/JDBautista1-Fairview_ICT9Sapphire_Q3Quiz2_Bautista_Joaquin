Synopsis

BROOKLYN NINE-NINE is a new single-camera ensemble comedy directed by Dan Goor and Michael Schur, and starring Andy Samberg and Andre Braugher. The film follows Detective Jake Peralta, a talented but carefree detective who is entrusted with the task of chasing down criminals. The precinct's new commanding officer, Captain Ray Holt, reminds Jake to respect the badge. Detective Amy Santiago, who grew up with seven brothers who were all cops, is close behind, aware of the number of arrests she needs to close the gap. Sergeant Terry Jeffords, a linebacker, loses his nerve due to his wife's twin baby girls. Detective Charles Boyle, who idolizes Jake, is the precinct's workhorse, despite not being brilliant or physically gifted. Charles pines for Detective Rosa Diaz, who is both tough and sexy, and is vocally opinionated about everything. Gina Linetti, the eccentric office manager, cleans up everyone's mess.

The film interrogates suspects, arrests perps, and solves murders, but ultimately, it is a workplace comedy that focuses on the men and women behind the badge, singing karaoke, grabbing a beer, and hitting on each other while protecting the fine people of Brooklyn.
